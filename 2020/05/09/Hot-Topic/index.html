Kim Il Sung’s cult of personality

The person our group choose is Kim Il sung. He was the main leader of the founding of the Democratic People’s Republic of Korea, as well as the founder of the Korean labor party. There were two reasons that made him the leader of North Korea. The first reason was his outstanding performance in the Japanese war. He won many victories with the Chinese army in more than 10 battles, thus gaining the trust and appreciation of the Soviet Union. On February 16, 1942, Kim Il Sung became the first battalion commander, and the Soviet Union awarded him the position of captain. (Britannica, 2009) His excellent command of the army laid the foundation for him to become a leader. The second reason was that Kim Il Sung won the Korean War. He put in more than 260000 soldiers in the war and cooperated with China and the Soviet Union to defeat the United Nations. (Britannica, 2018) The victory of the war made the North Korean people think that Kim Il Sung was an extremely great militarist and outstanding national leader. The Korean people’s worship of Kim Il Sung had reached the peak. After Kim Il Sung gained power, he put forward the basic line of post-war economic construction and vigorously promoted the socialist transformation of production relations in urban and rural areas. (Britannica, 2009) He used the idea of Marxism–Leninism. Kim Il Sung wanted to wipe out the socialist forces in North Korea. Like Stalin, Kim Il Sung began to clean up his opponents and political parties after the Korean War in order to maintain his total control. In 1951, Mu Jong, the leader of Yanan faction, was dismissed on the grounds of “Pyongyang lost” and “ineffective combat”. (Britannica, 2018) In 1956, influenced by Khrushchev of the Soviet Union, Pu Changyu of the Soviet Union and TSE Chianguikku of the Yanan school openly challenged Kim Il Sung. They accused the cult of personality of Kim Il sung. But they didn’t get the support, and then Kim Il Sung wiped out the two opposition parties for this reason. (Sommerlad, 2018) All the regime in North Korea was in his hands.

Compare with Stalin, Kim Il sung acted almost the same as him. First off, both of them used media to do propaganda so that everybody would know their greatness. Kim Il sung made laws that all major publications were to include “words of instruction” from Kim Il-sung and his name must be on the first line. (Sommerlad, 2020) It was the same as Stalin controls the ‘Pravda’. (Wilson, 2) Secondly, both of them gave themselves plenty of honorary names. Kim had the “Sun”, “Heavenly Leader”, “Great chairman” and the “Double Hero Gold Medal”. (Sommerlad, 2018) These kinds of title to strengthen his image of God in the hearts of the people. Thirdly, they all used violence to get rid of their opponents. Many government officials opposed to Kim Il Sung were assassinated, such as TSE chianguikku. He was found dead on the farm in 1961. Stalin created the famous NKVD to assassinate people who said bad word to Stalin. (Wilson 4) The final point was that they all change the official history according to their willing. In Kim Il Sung’s revised history of North Korea, Kim Il Sung came from a long history of leadership. In the process of fighting with Japan, he almost single-handedly defeated the Japanese army (without the help of the Soviet Union and China). Stalin also adapted history. (Sommerlad, 2020) He said “He takes on the mantle of Lenin’s disciple and servant of the party”. (Wilson 11）He declared to the people that he was the contemporary Lenin.
