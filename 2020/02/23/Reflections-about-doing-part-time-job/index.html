Reflections about doing part-time job

发表于 2020-02-23 | | 阅读数

During the first semester of my second year in high school, I had a lot of free time. So I found a famous restaurant in Nanjing as a part-time job. I do eight hours of part-time work every Saturday. My job is to simply deliver food and clear the table. I didn’t think it would be difficult at first. But when it came down to it, it was really painful.

First of all, some guests like to order a lot of food. This made the size of the tableware he used different, which caused me a lot of trouble in cleaning the table. Moreover, some food scraps will be scattered on the table, and I have to clean them carefully with napkins. The second is the workload. Saturday is a time for many people to go out. The restaurant where I work is in the mall, so the population is even greater. There are peaks at noon and at night. I didn’t have time to take a rest in that hour or two. I was working all the time. It’s a very common phenomenon that the desk can’t be cleaned up on time. What makes me tired is that I only have one hour of rest a day, and I have to stand and take care of the guests for the rest of the day. Every time I get off work, my feet ache.

After experiencing the hard work of the working people at the grassroots level in China, I learned how happy I was to be able to go to school. In the service industry, the payment of service personnel is not directly proportional to their wages. They do the same job every day, working late into the night with little pay. In order to avoid this situation in my future, I must study hard, be a mental worker, and make more contributions to the society.
