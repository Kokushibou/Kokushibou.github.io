Plan in space

Scientists are trying to recreate an Earth-like environment or a biosphere / mini-ecosystem. There are food webs that exist in a biosphere. You must choose at least 5 types of organisms (at least 3 of them must be plants) other than human beings to maintain the foundation of a mini- ecosystem.

- what type of organisms?

Vallisneria Natans (Producer): Absorb animal waste; Oxygen supply: the greatest function of water and grass. Under the light, the water grass continuously pours oxygen into the water and also into the bottom layer

Dinoflagellate Genus (Producer): Dinoflagellates are widely distributed in fresh water, brackish water and sea water; Dinoflagellate can reproduce in a large amount in a short period of time, and it is the main bait of marine animals like diatom

Euphausia Genus (Primary Consumer): Krill is an important bait for many fish and baleen whales; They mainly eat phytoplankton.

Alosa elongata (Secondary Consumer): Omnivorous animal; Can survive in both fresh and saltwater environments.

Photosynthetic bacteria (Decomposer): It has strong adaptability and can maintain the ecosystem through large-scale reproduction when the ecosystem is out of balance; It can decompose wastes generated by animals and transform them into simple inorganic substances, which can be released into the environment for the producers to use again

- biosphere setting

Where? In water. We would dig a small artificial lake with water proof material between water and the moon surface on the north pole of moon with a sealed, nonopaque greenhouse on the top of it, which is completely sealed and contains oxygen in it. The water can contain some mineral substances that moon soil contains, like iron ion or zinc ion.

Temperature? The water temperature should be constant, about 17.4 ℃, because all the beings that we choose require a constant temperature around 17.4℃. There will be solar thermostat system in the green house to control the temperature.

Gravity? The gravity on moon is about 1/6 of earth gravity, which is around 1.63 N/kg, but our location is at the north pole of moon, which gives a larger gravity than other places on moon. The gravity at the north pole on moon would be between 1.7 N/kg to 1.9 N/kg. Such gravity would be enough for days without wind, but if there are strong storms, water might be blown away, so we have a sealed space for the biosphere so that it would not be affected by wind.

Why this area? We chose to build an artificial lake at the north pole of moon with a greenhouse on the top of it to create a sealed space. The reason that we chose an artificial lake is that it would be easier for a marine ecosystem to survive since it requires less elements than terrestrial ecosystem. For example, soil must contain nitrogen phosphorus and potassium in order to make it easier for plants to survive. However, in water, all what we need are just sunlight for photosynthesis, oxygen and a complete eco-chain with producer, consumer and decomposer.

- materials needed

Bottom material: Polystyrene foam board.

We’re going to clip the foam board between two layers of rubber membranes. It is one of the three common internal wall insulation materials, which is a white object heated by the heated polystyrene beads containing volatile liquid foaming agent after heating. It can effectively keep the system warm.

Materials around and on top of the greenhouse: Particle Cloud.

Using a few grams of hydrogen to form a particle cloud between two layers of rubber membranes. It can significantly reduce space radiation problems. The hydrogen carried is decomposed into protons and electrons, which are sent to space, forming a plasma cloud around the spacecraft and establishing a power grid to attract the plasma cloud, so as to prevent it from leaving the spacecraft. The particle cloud would maintain its shape when it is electrified, so it must be connected to a solar generator.

Supporting material: Rebar, Screw etc.

- soil composition (Earth-like environment)

how to create it? Use Lunar soil and add some nutrition and the composition of water.

a. Direct transporting earth soil, water, and oxygen to the Moon are ineffective and expensive because it takes much space.
b. We can produce oxygen and water on the Moon; thus, we do not need to bring that much

from Earth. The steps to make oxygen and water can be roughly divided into the following:
1). The solar wind is made up of a stream of electrons; thus, when it crosses the surface of th-e Moon, it reacts with proton and creates Hydrogen. Since hydrogen atom is only made up of one proton and one electron, it can be created easily.
2). Silicon dioxide on the surface of the Moon decomposes and produces silicon and oxygen.
3). The oxygen and Hydrogen can react and produce water
This process can produce not only water but also oxygen, so the oxygen and water problem are solved.

What nutrients, elements, etc. does the soil need?

a. Coral sand, coarse sand, gravel sand, dolomite, and other materials are excellent substrate. The gap between these sandmolecules is large, which is conducive to the growth of the

roots of aquatic grass and can keep the water clean, which plays a role in purifying water

quality.

b. Weak acidic river sand.

c. The soil must contain some minerals such as C, H, O, N, P, K, Ca, Mg, Zn, Fe, Mb, Cu, B, and Cl so that Vallisneria natans can grow underwater. Meanwhile, the lunar soil has miner-als like Fe and Zn, so we only need to transport earth soil with remaining missing elements.

How will it be maintained?

- We build Solar thermostat, water facility and Solar power supply facility

- In this ecosystem, the aquatic plant(Vallisneria natans) can do photosynthesis and produce oxygen. Dinoflagellate can get energy through photosynthesis and reproduce very fast. They are the food source of euphausiids as well. Then, Alosa can get food from euphausiid. When Alosa dies, the Photosynthetic bacteria can decompose the wreckage of the fish and transform them into pure inorganic substances that can be used by producers again.

- Rocket mission logistics

Equipment: Polystyrene foam board, Particle Cloud (Power up after being assembled), Rebar, Screw, solar panel, electric generator, Nomo-thermal System and living things that we need

Cost: 350 million dollars in total, including equipment and the cost of a single rocket launch. (Falcon heavy launcher)

Mass: 1420 tones (Take-off weight of rocket)

​

- Is it better to use smaller rockets and transport many times or a large rocket and transport less times?

For our project, we prefer to use a large rocket and transport less times. Because we do not have so many things to carry and a single trip can perfectly satisfy us. A Felcon Heavy Carrier’s maximum capacity is 60 tones, which is enough for us. Also, our materials for the lake and the greenhouse all have almost permanent shelf life, so we do not need to change materials in a short time.
